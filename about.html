About

BeeQTLdb is a manually curated QTL database that includes information about quantitative trait loci (QTL) detected in honeybee.

Linkage and association mapping were used to link genetic polymorphisms to phenotypic variation observed in individuals, colonies, and populations of honey bees. Information on identified genomic regions associated with various honey bee traits is scattered in the scientific literature. Results come from studies based on different methods and experimental populations, and the traits studied have been defined in different ways. Here, we collected quantitative trait loci (QTL) in honey bees associated with different traits (e.g., queen fertility, resistance to chalkbrood and varroosis, hygienic, grooming, foraging and defensive behaviours) from the literature and curated them manually.

The database provides comprehensive information on QTL and allows us to combine results from multiple studies. Users can confirm or narrow down candidate genomic regions from overlapping QTL results. BeeQTLdb is designed to accelerate the interpretation of genotype-phenotype correlations and prioritisation of candidate genes in honey bee.
